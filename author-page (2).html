About Burke Campbell

Burke Campbell is Trade Currents' Senior Correspondent, specializing in international trade relations with a focus on Asia-Latin America connections. With over 15 years of experience covering global trade networks, Burke brings a unique perspective to complex trade issues, synthesizing economic data, policy developments, and on-the-ground reporting.

Prior to founding Trade Currents, Burke worked as a logistics consultant for multinational corporations operating in Latin America, providing strategic guidance on supply chain optimization and trade compliance. This hands-on industry experience informs his analytical approach to trade journalism.

Burke holds an MBA in International Business from UNITEC in Honduras and a Bachelor's degree in Economics from the University of British Columbia. He has lived and worked in both China and Honduras, gaining valuable insights into the cultural, economic, and political dynamics that shape trade relations between these regions.

His areas of expertise include:

  • International shipping logistics and supply chain management
  • Trade policy analysis with a critical political economy perspective
  • China's Belt and Road Initiative in Latin America
  • Regional economic integration in Central America
  • Emerging market trade dynamics

Burke's reporting has been recognized for its depth, clarity, and commitment to examining the power dynamics underlying international trade. He is fluent in English, Spanish, and Mandarin Chinese, enabling him to conduct original research and interviews across multiple markets.

Based in San Pedro Sula, Honduras, Burke frequently travels throughout Asia and Latin America to provide first-hand reporting on evolving trade relationships and their impacts on local economies.
